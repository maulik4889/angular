Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs) about Healthcare in London

Yes, England has a public Health system that is free, referred to as the NHS (National Health Services)

General Practitioner (GP) and nurse consultations or treatment in primary care are free of charge to all.


For secondary care services, you must be living lawfully in the UK on a properly settled basis to be entitled to free healthcare, otherwise you may be charged.


Short-term visitors from countries who have healthcare agreements with the UK can continue to access medically necessary healthcare. This also applies to EU visitors with a European Health Insurance Card (EHIC).


How to sort out your Healthcare in London

Register with a GP (General Practitioner)

When you arrive to England, you will need to register with your local GP, generally the one closest to your place of residence. This will allow you to have easy access to NHS services when you need them.

The GP (General Practitioners) surgeries are usually the first contact if you have a health problem. They can treat many conditions and give health advice. They can also refer you to other NHS services.

Search for “Health Centre” on Google Maps to find GPs near you

Depending on the GP, you may be able to register via different methods.
They may ask you for personal details and to fill in this form.
